Healthy Relationships Therapy is a therapeutical private practice owned and run by a single therapist, Angelina Puffelis. Hrt will provide clients with a safe and comfortable environment that allows each individual to share the need of their current situation during each session.

A healthy relationship is considered when two people, through constant engagement, develop a connection founded on mutual trust, respect, and open honest communication. In other words, a healthy relationship feels safe enough to be vulnerable when expressing oneself and no person feels as they are walking on egg shells.

Hrt’s goal is to help each individual successfully find solutions to their problems on their own, alongside the supportive guidance they need. In addition, Angelina is passionate in helping each person gain insight to create and reach their own goals resulting in a healthier future. Simply, hrt strives to help you in any possible way that it can, while making sure the journey to the better you was a pleasing and memorable one.

FAQ

  • Is therapy for me ?

    People usually seek therapy for various reasons such as relationship issues, parenting, work, money, life stages, divorce, grief, trauma and others. However, we have the tendency not to seek therapy due to the embarrassment or shame of reaching out to seek help. Unfortunately, therapy has been misunderstood and seen negative rather than positive. Therapy is a tool that helps resolve a wide range of issues. Ultimately, a person who seeks therapy is wise and demonstrates a sense of wisdom and healthier lifestyle than he or she knows.

  • How private is therapy really?

    Your information will be confidential. If an unauthorized person requests information about you, the therapist will decline to acknowledge you are a client. However, you may consent for your information to be released to a specific entity. Likewise, there are instances where the law requires your therapist to report any child abuse, self-harm, and elderly or dependent adult abuse. These technicalities will be addressed in further detail during the first sessions.

  • Is insurance available?

    Insurance will be accepted but is currently pending. You can find more information in pricing section above.

  • What do I need to bring to therapy?

    Yes, an intake form has been provided for you so you can have it filled out before the first appointment.This intake form can be found when clicking on the “client form” at the bottoom right under the services section. Also, If you have any previous evaluations / assessments, for you, or your child, by any previous medical professional please bring that as well.

  • How long are therapy sessions?

    Therapy sessions usually last 50 minutes, unless advised otherwse group sessions may last longer if all parties have come to an agreement on what works best.

  • What if I need to miss or cancel?

    As stated in the pricing section, clients who miss or cancel an appointment with less than a 24 hour notice, will be charged a fee of $50.00. Again, thank you for understanding.

  • How often do I need to go to therapy?

    Each case is unique and may require different accommodations depending on the need(s), and goal(s) in progress. Some clients may need to only meet once or some a couple times. Although it is recommended to finish the suggested amount of therapy sessions, clients are not forced to continue and may stop at anytime.

  • Can a young child benefit from therapy?

    Many therapists are skilled and use play therapy. Young children communicate through the universal language of play, since they temporarily have limited verbal skills. Parents are usually involved in sessions and provided with skills to help their child.
